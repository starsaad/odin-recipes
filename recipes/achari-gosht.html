Achari Gosht

# Description:

Achari Gosht is a flavorful and aromatic Indian-Pakistani meat dish known for its bold, tangy taste inspired by traditional pickling spices.
Made with tender pieces of mutton or beef, it is simmered in a rich, spicy gravy that features a unique blend of ingredients like fennel seeds,
mustard seeds, nigella seeds (kalonji), fenugreek, and dried red chilies—common in South Asian pickles (achar). Yogurt adds creaminess and balances
the heat, while a splash of vinegar or lemon juice enhances its signature tang. Often served with naan or steamed rice, Achari Gosht offers a punchy,
comforting experience that combines the zest of pickles with the heartiness of slow-cooked meat.

# Ingredients required:

  • 500g mutton or beef
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tsp achari spice mix (fennel, mustard, nigella seeds)
  • Salt, chili powder, turmeric

# How to make Achari Gosht:

  1. Heat oil in a pot and sauté sliced onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste; cook until aromatic.
  3. Add meat and sear until it changes color.
  4. Mix in yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, and salt.
  5. Add achari spices (fennel, mustard, nigella seeds); cook for a few minutes.
  6. Stir in chopped tomatoes or a splash of water, cover, and simmer until meat is tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with naan or rice.
